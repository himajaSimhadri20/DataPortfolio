Summary:My journey of analyzing coffee sales data from January 2019 to July 2022. The data encompasses sales across four major coffee brands (Arabica, Excelsa, Liberica, Robusta) with variations in roast type (Dark, Light, Medium), size (0.2kg, 0.5kg, 1.0kg, 2.5kg), and sales regions (United States, Ireland, and United Kingdom). The primary objective was to gain deeper insights into sales trends, identify key performance indicators, and uncover opportunities for business growth.

Data Preparation: The initial dataset required significant cleaning and formatting. This involved:
• Handling Missing Values: Addressing missing values by either imputation or removal, ensuring data integrity.
• Data Consistency: Correcting inconsistencies in data formats, brand names, and order dates to maintain data accuracy.
• Data Transformation: Creating new calculated fields such as unit price and total revenue, and aggregating data by month, region, and other relevant dimensions.
• Data Validation: Implementing checks to ensure data accuracy and identify any potential errors or anomalies.

Data Analysis and Visualization:
The analysis focused on the following key areas:
• Total Sales Over Time:
○ Analyzed overall sales trends from January 2019 to July 2022 using a line graph.
○ Observed inconsistent sales performance across different coffee brands throughout the period.
• Top Customers:
○ Identified the top 5 customers based on their sales volume using a bar chart.
Determined that Allis Willmore was the top customer, followed by Brenn Dunderdge, Terri Farra, Nealson Cuttler, and Don Filntiff.
Key Findings: • Inconsistent Sales Trends: Observed inconsistent sales performance across different coffee brands throughout the analysis period.
• Dominant US Market: The United States demonstrated the highest sales volume compared to Ireland and the United Kingdom.
• Top Customer Performance: Allis Willmore emerged as the top customer with significant sales volume.
Dashboard Development: The findings were presented in an interactive dashboard, enabling stakeholders to easily explore the data and gain deeper insights. The dashboard included:
• Interactive Visualizations: Line graphs, bar charts, and potentially other relevant visualizations to showcase sales trends, regional performance, and customer data.
• Data Filters: Interactive filters to allow users to segment data by brand, region, time period, and other dimensions.
• Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): Prominent display of key performance indicators such as total sales, sales growth, and top-selling products.
Assumptions and Caveats: • Data Completeness and Accuracy: Assumed the accuracy and completeness of the sales data.
• External Factors: Did not explicitly consider external factors such as economic conditions, competitor pricing, and marketing campaigns that could influence sales.
• Limited Time Frame: The analysis covers a specific period, and recent market trends or unforeseen events may not be reflected.
• Customer Segmentation: Limited insights into customer segmentation beyond the top 5 customers.
• Currency Fluctuations: Potential impact of currency fluctuations on sales comparisons across regions.
Conclusions: This project provided valuable insights into coffee sales trends and identified key areas for improvement. The dashboard serves as a valuable tool for stakeholders to monitor sales performance, identify opportunities, and make data-driven decisions to enhance business growth.
